Liquid epoxy resins typically feature lower viscosity, enhancing their flowability and penetration into substrates, making them ideal for applications like coatings and adhesives. Solid epoxy resins, on the other hand, exhibit higher viscosity, offering greater structural strength and durability in applications such as casting and molding. Both types cater to diverse industries, including automotive, construction, and electronics, driven by their unique properties and performance characteristics.</p></p>
