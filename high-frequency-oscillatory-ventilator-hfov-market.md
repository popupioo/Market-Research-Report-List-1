<p><p>High-Frequency Oscillatory Ventilation (HFOV) is a specialized method of mechanical ventilation that uses rapid oscillations to maintain alveolar recruitment and improve gas exchange. The market for HFOV is divided into invasive and non-invasive types. Invasive HFOV involves the use of an endotracheal tube for direct airway access, tailored for critically ill patients requiring advanced respiratory support. Non-invasive HFOV employs interfaces like nasal masks or helmets, providing respiratory assistance with reduced risks associated with intubation, suitable for less severe cases.</p></p>
