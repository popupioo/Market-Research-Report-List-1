<p><p>The nano-cellulose fibre market comprises three main types: Cellulose Nano Fibrils (CNF), Cellulose Nano Crystals (CNC), and Bacterial Nanocellulose (BNC). CNF consists of entangled fibres providing high strength and flexibility, making it suitable for various applications. CNC features crystalline regions that enhance mechanical properties and transparency, often used in cosmetics and packaging. BNC, produced by bacteria, is characterized by its purity and biocompatibility, finding use in medical and food sectors. Each type offers unique benefits tailored to specific industrial needs.</p></p>
