<p><p>The wearable sensors market is divided into two main types: invasive and non-invasive sensors. Invasive sensors are placed within the body to monitor physiological parameters, providing accurate and continuous data, often used in medical applications such as glucose monitoring and cardiac health. In contrast, non-invasive wearable sensors are worn externally, such as smartwatches and fitness trackers, to measure metrics like heart rate, activity levels, and sleep patterns. Both types cater to different user needs, ranging from health management to fitness tracking.</p></p>
