<p><p>Negative Pressure Wound Therapy (NPWT) is a medical treatment that involves the application of sub-atmospheric pressure to a wound, promoting healing by enhancing blood flow, reducing edema, and preventing infection. NPWT devices typically consist of a sealed dressing over the wound, connected to a vacuum pump that creates negative pressure. This therapy is employed for various wounds, including chronic ulcers, surgical sites, and traumatic injuries.</p><p>The NPWT market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing prevalence of chronic wounds, rising geriatric populations, and a growing focus on advanced wound care technologies. As healthcare providers recognize the efficacy of NPWT in expediting recovery and reducing complications, adoption rates are rising.
